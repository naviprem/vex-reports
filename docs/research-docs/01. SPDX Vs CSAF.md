For your B2B enterprise software team, choosing between CSAF and SPDX for VEX (Vulnerability Exploitability eXchange) reports depends largely on whether your priority is **high-fidelity advisory communication** or **integrated supply chain inventory**.

Below is a comparison to help you communicate these choices to your customers.

### Comparison Overview

|**Feature**|**CSAF (Common Security Advisory Framework)**|**SPDX (Software Package Data Exchange)**|
|---|---|---|
|**Primary Focus**|Security advisories and formal vulnerability disclosure.|Holistic software supply chain transparency (SBOM + Security).|
|**Philosophy**|"Security-first": Designed for clear communication of risk and mitigation.|"Relationship-based": Vulnerability data is a layer within a broader graph of software components.|
|**Best For**|Regulated industries, enterprise security teams, and formal vendor-to-customer advisory workflows.|Organizations already using SPDX for SBOM management who want to "enrich" that data with security status.|
|**Complexity**|High (Powerful but can be complex to generate/validate).|Moderate (Requires managing relationships within a larger graph).|
|**Alignment**|Strong alignment with global regulatory requirements (e.g., EU CRA).|Flexible; maps well to other formats; supports "round-tripping" data.|

### Key Considerations for Your Customers

#### 1. Why CSAF is often the "Gold Standard" for B2B Enterprise

If your customers are in highly regulated sectors (Finance, Healthcare, Government), they are likely looking for **CSAF**.

- **Purpose-Built for Advisories:** It is specifically designed to answer "How does this CVE affect your product?" with clear, actionable fields for mitigations, justifications, and remediation timelines.
    
- **Industry Adoption:** It is widely recognized by major software vendors for publishing security bulletins, making it highly "trustworthy" for enterprise security operations centers (SOCs).
    
- **Regulatory Alignment:** Its rigid structure makes it ideal for audit and compliance requirements.
    

#### 2. Why SPDX is the "Integration Choice"

If your customers are primarily focused on **SBOM-centric workflows**, SPDX may be the more natural fit.

- **Integrated Context:** Since SPDX is primarily an SBOM standard, embedding VEX data (using the Security Profile in SPDX 3.0) allows customers to see the vulnerability status _directly alongside_ the component metadata.
    
- **Unified Graph:** It excels in environments where you need to track dependencies, licenses, and security status as a single, cohesive map of the software.
    
- **Interoperability:** SPDX is designed to be a "spec of specs," meaning it can ingest and map data from other formats, providing a "single pane of glass" for supply chain management.
    

### Recommendation for Your Team

- **If you want to be "Enterprise-Ready":** Adopt **CSAF**. It is the industry benchmark for security disclosures. Your enterprise customers will likely have tooling already in place to ingest CSAF files.
    
- **If you want to be "Supply-Chain-Integrated":** Use **SPDX 3.0**. It offers a modern, graph-based approach that is gaining traction as supply chain security moves beyond simple vulnerability scanning toward comprehensive BOM management.
    

**Pro-Tip for Customers:** You don't necessarily have to choose just one. Many enterprise-grade solutions generate both: CSAF for direct security communications to customers, and an SPDX-formatted SBOM (with VEX/Security profile) for deeper architectural and compliance analysis.
